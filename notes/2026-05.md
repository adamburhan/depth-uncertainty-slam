## 2026-05-07
Discovered that pyslam's pose optimization uses chi²-threshold outlier classification. Uniform attenuation of invSigma2 (multiply by 0.5) achieves comparable tracking loss reduction to principled depth-weighting on mannequin_4 (3/4 vs 4/4 at 0% loss). The tracking-robustness gain is partially or wholly explained by the chi² interaction rather than the depth-uncertainty signal.

On table\_7 the results aren't affected since on both baseline and depth version tracking was not lost, however should confirm that uniform attenuation does not also have similar results as depth version.

## 2026-05-14
Launched the large-scale TartanAir sweep: 18 environments × 7 presets (baseline, depth_gt λ∈{0.01,0.1,1.0}, depth_est λ∈{0.01,0.1,1.0}) × ~3–10 sequences × 10 seeds = 126 SLURM jobs. Did a full diff audit of pyslam fork against the initial commit. Depth-weighting code (bucket 1) is clean. Infra changes (bucket 2) are inert. One unintended finding: USE_CPP_CORE = False has been active for all runs — the entire Python core is being used instead of the C++ backend. Relative comparisons within the sweep are still valid (all presets share the same backend), but absolute numbers may differ from C++ runs. Plan: let the sweep finish, then run a small C++/Python sanity comparison on 2–3 sequences to decide if a C++ rerun is needed before the paper. Other bucket-3 changes (deterministic ORB, cuDNN flags, tracking-lost counter includes RELOCALIZE) are all intentional or harmless.
Also confirmed the mean-1 normalization approach addresses the chi² confound from May 7 — weights redistribute influence rather than globally loosening outlier rejection.
